# Assistive-AAC-Prototype

I started with a breadboard AAC device and will work with a Speech Language Pathologist to upgrade and innovate it, bridging the gap between the cost & power of current technology and the cost & power of existing dedicated speech-generating devices.

Problem Statement & Background:

Not every individual communicates through spoken language. Be it because of trauma, disability, or sometimes preference, humans have been shown to create/innovate new methodologies of communicating with one another. As the husband of a Speech Language Pathologist, I have learned much about Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) and the long-term benefits that such devices provide for the non-speaking community.

Unfortunately, for individuals with complex needs, dedicated Speech-Generating Devices(SGD) can cost between $3,000 and $15,000. Tobii Dynavox, a leading company in SGDs, has an advanced model named I-Series that can cost upwards of $8,000 if eye gaze technology is needed for the individual. These prices are exponentially higher than what most people would expect to pay for a sophisticated computer capable of running virtualizations of these same devices. Even AAC apps can cost $300 alone or a constant monthly subscription, not including the cost of the tablet itself [1]. Could you imagine having your ability to communicate locked behind a paywall?

As technology advances and becomes more efficient, it is the responsibility of society to ensure that medical equipment for the Disabled community does not fall behind or become cost-prohibitive for the general public. Currently, 28.7% of adults have some form of disability [2]. Adults between the ages of 20 and normal retirement age have a 1 in 4 chance of becoming disabled before they retire [3]. This metric only increases as adults get older. By utilizing the social model of disability, we can differentiate between an impairment, such as not having developed muscles to produce speech sounds, and what is a disability; the individual cannot communicate because they lack access to an affordable AAC device. From there, a better quality of life is available to the individual because of the bridged gap of understanding.

Hardware:

1x Breadboard;
1x ELEGOO UNO R3 Controller Board;
1x 16x2 LCD Screen;
5x Buttons;
1x Potentiometer;
5x 10K resistors;
5x 104pF capicitors;
26x Jumper Wires of varying lengths

Current Status:

Currently in the CAD/prototype phase, utilizing the breadboard to embed code into the control board, and transforming the components into CAD models on Autodesk Fusion.
